In my Spring 2019 programming course, we were given the flexibility to choose our own project to do. I decided I wanted to create my version of the game Pong.

My game has the option of single-player or two-player if you want to play with a friend. The sinlge player option pits you against a simple computer opponent that identifies where the ball is at and then moves a variable amount to try and intercept it.

In addition to this, there are also several different themes users can chose from, each with their own style. I enjoyed doing this as it allowed me to express my creativity.

In my database course, throughout the course of the semester, myself and two groupmates worked to design a datavase from beginning to finish.

We began with the conceptual design portion of our database. This involved designing an Entity Relationship Diagram as well as defining any assumptions we made.

Next we moved onto logical design. In this portion of our project we worked to normalize our tables to reduce redundancy, eliminate data duplication, and ensure well-structured relations by making columns atomic. For our project, we used the third normal form of normalization. During this stage, we also ensured referential integrity, defining relationships between our associations.

The final step of our project was physical design and implementation. Here is were we created a data dictionary and wrote out our create statements to create our tables and relationships between the tables. We also inserted in random data we generated through a website. After creating our database and entering data, we would enter some SQL select statements to ensure our database was working properly.

This project was beneficial as we got first hand experience in all of the innerworkings of a database, and how tricky it can be if careful consideration is not taken for each relationship.
